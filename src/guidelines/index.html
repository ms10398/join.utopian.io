How to contribute

Use your favourite STEEM frontend Steemit.com or Busy.org and publish your contributions or task requests by following our Guidelines.
  • Set the first tag of your post: utopian-io
  • Set the second tag of your post based on the category. Each category tag can be found below in the category specific guidelines.
  • In the body of your post always include the link to the github repository at the top, i.e. https://github.com/utopian-io/utopian.io
  • Follow any of the predefined templates to make sure your contribution has a clear format. Each category template can be found below in the category specific guidelines.
  • Once your contribution or task request has been submitted wait up to 72 hours for a Moderator to review it.
  • If your contribution is eligible for an upvote, the main @utopian-io account will vote and comment on your post.

Policies vs Guidelines

Policies-Mandatory rules for use of Utopian services and participation in the community. Failing to adhere to one or more of the policies will lead to the immediate rejection of your submission, and may lead to a downvote and / or ban from Utopian services.

Guidelines- General guidelines for submission of contributions to Utopian. Failing to adhere to guidelines may lead to a lower score of your submission, thus lowering its chances of receiving a reward.

Bans and Downvotes Policy

The following may lead to a temporary or permanent ban from Utopian:

  • Failing to adhere to Utopian Policies.
  • Harassing any member of the Utopian team.
  • Using multiple accounts to abuse the system.
  • Plagiarism.
  • Spamming (including tag spamming).

Vote and Approval Retraction Policy

  • Submissions by users found in violation of Utopian policies can be unvoted even after they have been reviewed and scored.
  • Erroneous (accidental) Utopian upvotes may be retracted.

Forbidden Content Policy


Commercial / Copyrighted Materials

  • Images and videos used must be distributed under the Creative Commons license and content source must be cited (when applicable).
  • Inclusion of licensed or commercial materials may lead to a downvote and/or temporary or permanent ban from Utopian services.

Spam & Solicitation

  • Contributions submitted for monetary gain alone, or ones that appear as obvious attempts to monetize redundant efforts via Utopian, will not be eligible for reward. Continuous submission of such contributions may lead to a downvote and/or temporary or permanent ban from Utopian services.
  • Tag spamming or any kind of spam content will not be reviewed, and may lead to a downvote and/or temporary or permanent ban from Utopian services.
  • Submissions that solicit any activity that is not allowed according to Utopian Policies will not be eligible for reward.

Defamation

  • Contribution content and submission posts to Utopian must not include any kind of derogatory or offensive speech directed at persons, groups, beliefs or political views.
  • Contribution content, submission posts to Utopian and comments must not contain false information about another user that may be perceived as defamatory.

Github Integration Policy

To be considered for reward Utopian contributors must have an active GitHub account associated with their Utopian / Steem account (unless otherwise specified).

  • Disrespectful communication with Project Owners on GitHub, mention of Utopian on GitHub services, or any unsolicited representation of Utopian on external platforms that may be harmful to the Utopian brand will result in an immediate and permanent ban from all Utopian services.
  • All contributions submitted must be linked to an open source project with a GitHub repository.
  • The Github repository linked to the submission post must contain the project’s source code, a readme file with usage and install instructions and an appropriate open source license, or be directly connected to such a repository.
  • Contributions to un-official repositories will only be eligible for potential reward if the repository is listed in Utopian un-official repos whitelist.
  • Contributions to repositories listed on Utopian repos blacklist will not be eligible for potential reward.
  • Contributions to official repositories that are mirrors of another subversioning system are eligible for potential reward.
  • Contributions to repositories that have not received any program code updates for longer than 6 months, will not be eligible for potential reward.
  • Contributions to forks that do not have any difference / improvement over the original project will not eligible for potential reward.
  • Contributions to own projects that do not accept contributions from other users will not be eligible for potential reward.

Post Submission Guidelines


Submission Content & Format

  • Inclusion of any or all of the following may lead to a lower score of your submission:
    • Links to commercial products and affiliate links
    • Requests for Steem / Steemit engagement (upvotes, resteems and follows)
    • Tagging or mentioning other Steem / Utopian users without valid reason
    • Including links to social profiles in a disruptive way
    • Asking for Steem witness votes in a disruptive way
  • To ensure contributions to Utopian are easy to read and understand, they should be written in a formal and professional style. Unprofessional, unclear, difficult to comprehend due to grammatical errors, or informally written submissions will receive a lower score or may not be reviewed at all. Please refer to the following resource when writing contribution submissions - https://owl.english.purdue.edu/owl/resource/656/02/
  • Only submissions in easily understood English will be reviewed and scored. Contributions in other languages must include a full and comprehensible translation of all content included in the submission.
  • To increase reliability and allow for efficient reviewing, please use the submission templates provided for each category. Feel free to edit and expand upon the templates as long as the formatting is clear and all the information necessary for review is included.
  • Images and videos used in contribution submissions should be of high resolution. Low resolution images and videos may lead to a lower score of your contribution.

Contribution Value, Volume and Detail

  • In order to be considered for a reward, the contribution must add value to the Open Source project. Submissions that offer little to no added value to the project will not be eligible for potential reward.
  • The submission should contain as much detail as necessary, describing the work done and (when possible) offering added value to readers.
  • When applicable, graphical content like images, charts, videos and screenshots should be included.
  • The length of the body of the contribution should be sufficient to provide a thorough overview of the submitted contribution.
  • Texts should be structured using markdown to ensure readability. If you are not well familiar with Markdown or selected HTML tags to make your post look good, you can read more about it in Mastering Markdown on GitHub or in this comprehensive guide by @espoem.

Submission Originality

All submitted contributions must be unique. To ensure your submission is eligible for potential reward, please check if an identical or very similar contribution was previously submitted.

  • Duplicate submissions (by the same or different user) will not be eligible for potential reward.
  • Content shared previously will not be considered for reward if a moderator discovers plagiarism, and the user submitting it will be banned from Utopian.
  • All submissions must be a direct result of the contributor’s own work. Modifying the work/assets of others, or using a template while claiming it as fully original work will not be considered for reward, and the user submitting it may be banned from Utopian.

Contribution Authorship

  • To be eligible for potential reward, the submission post must include the information necessary to verify the identity of the contributor as the post author.
  • If a contributor’s Steem / Utopian username does not match the username used in an external platform, they should either edit the username on the external platform, or provide alternative means of verification.
  • Unless otherwise specified, every contributor should only submit ONE contribution post per task request. Use of multiple accounts to circumvent this rule will lead to immediate ban from Utopian services.

Task Request Submission Guidelines

Task requests are submitted by Open Source project owners seeking contributions to their project.

  • Submitted tasks should be explained in great detail and provide all the necessary details to actually be completed.
  • Every Task Request Submission post should include one task request only. Submissions with multiple tasks requests will not be considered for reward or promotion.
  • Task Request Submissions should be submitted to the category relevant to the type of request.
  • Generic Task Request Submissions like "We are looking for contributors" will not be considered for reward or promotion.
  • Task Request Submissions should include a means of communication for the contributor to contact the task publisher.
  • Task Request Submissions should specify a deadline.
  • Images, screenshots, links and examples should be included as part of the submission, when applicable.
  • Project owners submitting a task request must remain available for inquiry. If no response or input from the project owner was received in 2 days, the submission will be scored as-is and may receive a lower score, thus reducing its chances of reward and/or promotion.
